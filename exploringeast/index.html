Exploring EAST For You


  1. Darjeeling, West Bengal

    • Famous for: breathtaking views of the Himalayas, Darjeeling is a hill station renowned for its tea gardens, toy train, and colonial charm.
    • Best time to visit: March to May and October to November
    • Darjeeling, a hill station nestled in the Eastern Himalayas, is renowned for its breathtaking views of snow-capped mountains and lush tea gardens. Known as the "Queen of Hills," it offers a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Enjoy a ride on the iconic Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, explore vibrant markets, and savor the world-famous Darjeeling tea. This charming town is a perfect blend of natural beauty and colonial heritage.

  1. Shillong, Meghalaya

    • Famous for: "Scotland of the East," Shillong offers lush green landscapes, waterfalls, living root bridges, and a peaceful atmosphere
    • Best time to visit: March to May and October to November
    • Shillong, often called the "Scotland of the East," is a picturesque hill station in Meghalaya. Known for its lush greenery, waterfalls like Elephant Falls, and serene lakes like Umiam, it's a haven for nature lovers. Explore the living root bridges, a marvel of Khasi ingenuity, or simply relax amidst the cool, pleasant weather. Shillong offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle, promising a rejuvenating experience.

  1. Kaziranga National Park, Assam

    • Famous for: One-horned rhinoceros, Kaziranga is a wildlife enthusiast's paradise. It also offers opportunities for elephant safaris and bird watching.
    • Best time to visit: November to April
    • Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a wildlife haven in Assam. Famous for being home to the world's largest population of one-horned rhinos, it's a paradise for nature enthusiasts. Apart from rhinos, the park boasts diverse flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, and a variety of birdlife. Experience the thrill of a jeep safari, witnessing these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat.
